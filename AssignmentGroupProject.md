## **Problem Statement**

| 🎯 | Information overload is the primary constraint on knowledge work. The tool must not hallucinate citations — every claim in the output must trace to a specific source passage. |
| :---- | :---- |

## **What You're Building**

| 🔨 | A research synthesis platform: query-driven paper discovery, multi-document ingestion, claim extraction, cross-source comparison, and structured brief generation with traceable citations. |
| :---- | :---- |

## **Core Features**

* Paper Discovery – Given a research question, search academic sources (arXiv, Semantic Scholar API, or uploaded PDFs) and return a ranked list of relevant papers.

* Document Ingestion – Ingest full-text PDFs. Extract abstract, methodology, key findings, limitations, and conclusion as structured fields.

* Claim Extraction – Extract specific factual claims from each paper with source section and page reference. Distinguish findings from hypotheses from limitations.

* Cross-Source Synthesis – Group related claims across papers. Identify where sources agree, conflict, or where evidence is thin. Surface the strength of consensus.

* Research Brief Generation – Generate a structured brief: executive summary, key findings by theme, areas of consensus, open questions, and recommended next papers.

* Citation Traceability – Every claim in the output links to the specific paper and section it came from. No unsourced assertions. Support export to standard citation formats.
